What is Linux? And Why Should You Care?

C.J. Wade

Intro

In today’s world, we interact with computers and techonology every day. We place a large amount of trust in our computers and the operating systems that run on them.

But, we rarely stop and ask ourselves if the operating systems that we use are worthy of that trust. All your data is being harvested, not just online but through your company owned operating systems as well.

Thankfully, there are alternative operating systems that offer more than the standard that we are used to. Enter Linux.

A little about me, I am a Computer Science major who has personally used Linux fulltime starting from 2019. I also have used Windows from when I started using computers, with macOS not long after.

What is Linux?

  1. Linux is an open source kernel
    1. What is a kernel? [Kernel Definition, anonymous, 2004, https://www.linfo.org/kernel.html]
      • A kernel is the core of an operating system.
    2. Who wrote it? [Free minix-like kernel sources for 386-AT, Linus Torvalds, 1991, https://groups.google.com/g/comp.os.minix/c/4995SivOl9o/m/GwqLJlPSlCEJ?pli=1]
    3. What is open source? [Philosophy of the GNU Project - GNU Project - Free Software Foundation, Richard Stallman, 2023, https://www.gnu.org/philosophy/open-source-misses-the-point.html
      • When something is open source, then it means that the source code is available to be viewed by anyone.
  2. How do we make an operating system from a kernel? [Linux and GNU - GNU Project - Free Software Foundation, Richard Stallman, 2021, https://www.gnu.org/gnu/linux-and-gnu.en.html]
    1. GNU is the answer!
      • A software system compatible with Unix. (Long history short, Unix is one of the oldest operating systems that has incredible impact on all OS’s.)
    2. Who wrote it? [Initial Annoucement - GNU Project - Free Software Foundation, Richard Stallman, 1983, https://www.gnu.org/gnu/initial-announcement.html]
      • The GNU Project has had many contributors since its start in 1983, but it’s chief architect has been Richard Stallman.
    3. What it stands for. [The GNU Manifesto - GNU Project - Free Software Foundation, Richard Stallman, 1985, https://www.gnu.org/gnu/manifesto.html]
      1. GNU is short for GNU’s Not Unix.
      2. The mission of the GNU Project is to provide a freely available OS that is not constrained by the bottom line of a business, or by sacrificing the privacy of its users, or even by obscuring hidden functions in code that is not viewable by the end user.
      3. You might think that credit should be given to both projects when refering to them in their fused state, if so, you might get along well with Stallman who champions GNU/Linux as the proper name. If it didn’t occur to you, welcome to the rest of the world.
  3. What is Linux used for?
    1. Servers (via nginix & apache [September 2023 Web Server Survey | Netcraft, , 2023, https://www.netcraft.com/blog/september-2023-web-server-survey/]
      • In modern life, we use the Internet daily. Most of the websites are hosted on Linux servers using either the nginix or apache programs.
    2. Personal O.S. [Pop!_OS by System76, System76, 2023, https://pop.system76.com/]
      • Many people use Linux as a personal OS, one of my favorites is Pop!_OS, maintained by System76.

Why Should You Care?

  1. The Competition
    1. Windows
      • Windows is the defacto OS for most everyone on the planet.
    2. macOS
      • Chances are, if you’re not on Windows your on macOS.
  2. Privacy
    1. Windows [Has Windows become Spyware? - YouTube, https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IT4vDfA_4NI, YouTube, The PC Security Channel, 2023, Jan]
      • Windows is known to track most everything that its users does.
        • A useful tool for circumventing Microsoft’s spyware. [GitHub - ChrisTitusTech/winutil: Chris Titus Tech’s Windows Utility - Install Programs, Tweaks, Fixes, and Updates, Chris Titus, 2023, https://github.com/chrisTitusTech/winutil]
    2. macOS [Does Apple really log every app you run? A technical look - Jacopo Jannone - blog, Jacopo Jannone, 2020, https://blog.jacopo.io/en/post/apple-ocsp/]
      • Apple logs your IP address everytime you open an app.
  3. Freedom
    1. Windows
      • No one knows what Windows does under the hood outside of the people who authored/are authoring it.
    2. macOS
      • macOS is decended from BSD (an offshoot of UNIX from UC Berkeley), however the problem still persists, what it does under the hood is unknown to all but its developers.

Conclusion

All in all, Linux has a much greater emphasis on privacy. This emphasis comes from multiple sources, both the philosophy of Stallman when initiating the GNU Project, and from the people who work on it today. They have no need to harvest data from their own users, they just want to make something that they would enjoy using themselves.

Linux may not be for everyone, in fact, I will tell you that it’s not. However, it might be for you. Will you ever know if you don’t try?
